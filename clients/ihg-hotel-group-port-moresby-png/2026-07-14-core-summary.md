# CORE summary: IHG HOTEL Group - Port Moresby PNG

- **Date:** 2026-07-14
- **Org type:** org

## Belief

In a market where international standards are promised but rarely delivered, the only way to hold the premium position is to close the gap between the global brand promise and the daily local experience, one venue, one occasion, one trained team member at a time.

## Position

IHG Hotels Group Port Moresby is the only international hotel operator in Papua New Guinea with a multi-property, multi-tier portfolio capable of meeting an organisation's full accommodation and entertainment needs, from efficient business-travel rooms to premium extended-stay residences, all within a single managed relationship and backed by the security and service standards of a global system. In a market where the international promise is routinely overstated and under-delivered, IHG's competitive position rests on closing that gap through structured venue programming, staff capability investment, and a food and beverage activation strategy anchored to the occasions that matter most to the people already in the market.

## Customers

> Who we serve and what they are really buying, ranked by commercial pull, never listed as equals.

### Lead: Corporate and expat in-market patrons: business travellers, LNG and resource-sector professionals, conference delegates, and resident expats

- **Really buying:** Reliable security, globally consistent service standards, and a venue where they can eat, drink and entertain colleagues without the unpredictability of the broader Port Moresby market
- **Why lead:** This segment drives the highest volume of repeat spend, fills rooms across the working week, and represents the most controllable and incremental revenue base across accommodation, food and beverage, and meetings and events. The follow-up confirmed they are the bread-and-butter priority and that growing their average spend, not acquiring new customers, is the near-term profit lever.

### Scale: Leisure and tourist visitors, including NRL and major sporting event patrons

- **Really buying:** A safe, comfortable and socially credible base in Port Moresby with food and beverage occasions that feel worth the price of a meal or a round of drinks
- **Why scale:** Identified as a meaningful secondary revenue opportunity, particularly when activation is anchored to the NRL match calendar and local sporting occasions, which carry deep cultural resonance across PNG and can drive venue traffic on otherwise soft trading nights.


## Offer

> What we sell and how it is packaged: a tiered ladder, not a flat menu, with one clear reason to choose.

### Entry: Holiday Inn Port Moresby: Accessible Full-Service Stay

Mid-to-full-service accommodation with restaurant, bar and pool access, suited to budget-conscious business travellers, tour groups and independent travellers who need a secure, reliable base

*Why it sits here:* Brings guests into the IHG ecosystem at a lower price point and creates the first opportunity to upsell food and beverage spend and repeat visits across the portfolio

### Core: Holiday Inn Express Port Moresby: Business and Conference Hub

Efficient, safety-first accommodation with meetings and events facilities, conferencing, and food and beverage outlets calibrated for the corporate and delegate segment

*Why it sits here:* Anchors the highest-frequency, highest-volume commercial segment: in-market professionals who need consistent service, secure premises, and a functional venue for work-related entertaining and events

### Expansion: Crowne Plaza Residences Port Moresby: Premium Extended-Stay and Luxury Experience

Apartment-style residences with premium dining, cocktail bar, and a full-service lifestyle proposition for senior expats, long-stay resource-sector executives, and high-value leisure guests

*Why it sits here:* Captures the highest per-night and per-guest yield, extends the guest relationship from a single stay into a long-term accommodation and lifestyle relationship, and positions the group for the incoming InterContinental product at the top of the market

**One reason to choose:** IHG Port Moresby is the only operator in the country that can move a guest from a secure, affordable room to a premium residence to a five-star luxury hotel, all within the same trusted global system, making it the default choice for organisations that need to house and entertain staff at every level.

## Rivals

> Who we are really up against and where the ownable seam sits, backed by evidence, not asserted.

### Airways Hotel (5-star, airport-adjacent, Expedia rating 9.2, frequently cited as top-ranked in market)

- **Their claim:** The most convenient and highest-rated international-standard hotel in Port Moresby, preferred by travellers who want proximity to Jacksons International Airport and consistently strong service scores
- **Where it is weak:** A single-property operator with no multi-brand ladder, no extended-stay product, and no pathway to scale its offer as the PNG market grows. Its strength is location, not breadth.
- **Our response:** IHG Port Moresby is the only group in PNG with a three-property portfolio and a fourth under development, offering organisations a single operator relationship across accommodation tiers, conference facilities, and long-stay residences. Airways wins on proximity to the airport; IHG wins on programme depth and corporate account management.

### The Stanley Hotel and Suites (5-star, Vision City Mega Mall connection, Expedia rating 8.8)

- **Their claim:** Premium integrated lifestyle destination combining hotel accommodation with direct access to Port Moresby's largest retail and entertainment complex
- **Where it is weak:** The retail and mall connection is a convenience feature, not a security or service quality differentiator, and its ratings, whilst strong, sit below Airways. Its positioning is location-dependent and does not extend to a multi-property or extended-stay offer.
- **Our response:** IHG's garden and grounds setting, global loyalty programme, and the security of a recognised international brand system give corporate and expat patrons confidence that Stanley's mall proximity cannot replicate. For organisations managing duty-of-care obligations, the IHG brand system provides documented service and safety standards.

### Marriott Executive Apartments Port Moresby (extended-stay, direct competitor to Crowne Plaza Residences)

- **Their claim:** A globally recognised premium brand offering apartment-style long-stay accommodation for executives and expats in Port Moresby
- **Where it is weak:** Competes on the same Marriott Bonvoy loyalty infrastructure but, as a single-format product, cannot offer the tiered access to dining, conferencing and lifestyle venues that the IHG multi-property portfolio provides. Corporate accounts must split their accommodation arrangements across brands.
- **Our response:** Crowne Plaza Residences backed by the wider IHG Port Moresby portfolio means long-stay guests have access to multiple restaurants, event spaces and loyalty benefits under one managed relationship, reducing the administrative burden on corporate travel managers handling large PNG-based workforces.

### The internal capability gap: underperforming food and beverage activation and staff training shortfalls across all three properties

- **Their claim:** Not an external competitor, but the client has identified this as the primary commercial threat: the gap between the IHG global brand promise and the quality of local delivery is eroding venue traffic and average spend
- **Where it is weak:** Current activation is limited to social cocktail posts with no structured programming, no event calendar anchored to local occasions such as NRL fixtures, and no repeatable mechanism to drive mid-week venue traffic
- **Our response:** A structured food and beverage activation programme anchored to the NRL match calendar, local occasions and corporately relevant events, supported by staff capability investment, closes the gap between the global brand perception and the in-market experience, and creates a defensible local identity that external competitors cannot replicate by brand alone.


## Expression

> How the brand and business looks, sounds and behaves in market, held to one voice across every channel.

- **Voice:** Assured and internationally grounded but locally committed: the tone of an organisation that knows its global standards and holds itself accountable to them in a market that has been let down by half-measures before. Direct, clear and never boastful. Institutional confidence without institutional distance.
- **Traits:** Globally credentialled, locally accountable: every claim is backed by a standard, a process or a proof point, never asserted without substance, Calm authority: communications do not chase trends or shout for attention but lead with the premise that the right guests already know quality when they see it, Culturally present: the brand acknowledges the PNG context, the passions of the local market including rugby league, and the realities of operating in Port Moresby, rather than pretending to be a generic international hotel that could be anywhere
- **Generic test (we would never say):** We are committed to delivering exceptional experiences for all our guests.

## Killer questions

- If Airways Hotel consistently outscores IHG properties on guest-satisfaction aggregators, why would a corporate travel manager choose IHG as their preferred Port Moresby operator?
- The client has identified staff capability as the primary internal constraint. How does any marketing or activation programme hold its ground if the in-venue experience does not yet match the brand promise being made?
- NRL and sporting event activation is a strong cultural fit, but if the current agency retainer has not delivered a structured programme, what is the specific mechanism that makes the next proposal different and measurable?
- With the brief covering all three properties and a limited budget, which property or which customer segment gets activation priority in the first 90 days, and what is the rationale for that sequencing?
- The positioning relies on the multi-tier portfolio as a differentiator. How does IHG convert a guest who arrives via Holiday Inn Express into a Crowne Plaza Residences long-stay customer, and is there currently any cross-property commercial mechanism to do this?

## Decision

### Decided

- Corporate and expat in-market patrons are the lead commercial segment; tourist and leisure visitors are the secondary segment
- The offer is structured as a three-tier ladder: Holiday Inn (entry), Holiday Inn Express (core), Crowne Plaza Residences (expansion)
- Food and beverage activation is the lead priority for the next engagement, with average spend uplift from existing patrons the primary revenue objective rather than new customer acquisition
- Safety and security is the confirmed ownable positioning seam in the PNG market context
- NRL and major sporting events are the primary cultural activation vehicle for food and beverage venue programming
- The engagement covers all three properties collectively, with the recommendation that activation is sequenced or focused depending on budget allocation
- The internal capability gap, specifically staff training and structured programme delivery, is treated as a rival-equivalent commercial threat, not a background assumption

### Open

- Whether IHG Port Moresby's guest satisfaction scores on third-party aggregators have improved, declined or remained stable in the past 12 months, and how management is responding
- The specific budget envelope available for the next financial year activation programme beyond the current retainer range
- Which property or venue carries the highest food and beverage revenue potential and therefore the strongest case for lead activation investment
- Whether confirmed NRL international fixtures in PNG are secured and on what dates, which determines whether the sporting calendar is a firm activation platform or an aspirational one
- The status of any existing corporate account or preferred-supplier agreements with LNG and resource-sector organisations operating in Port Moresby
- Whether IHG Port Moresby has any local social media presence separate from global IHG brand channels, and what audience size and engagement baseline exists

### Required before design

- Confirmation that the brief covers the three-property portfolio as a collective entity and agreement on which property leads in activation sequencing
- Confirmation of the available budget for the new programme, separate from the current contracted retainer, to determine whether a full activation calendar or a phased pilot is the right scope
- Evidence to substantiate the positioning: the claim of being the largest international hotel operator in PNG requires independent confirmation before it is used in external brand or campaign materials
- Confirmation of NRL fixture dates in PNG or a clear read on the probability of those fixtures occurring within the activation window
- A baseline audit of current staff capability and training investment, so that any activation programme is calibrated to what the venues can actually deliver at launch
